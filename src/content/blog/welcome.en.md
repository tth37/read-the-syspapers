Welcome. This page is a placeholder — a working backstage pass to how blog posts here are written, published, and kept honest. Real essays will land here over time: each takes a single perspective a reader cares about and defends it using the conference papers indexed elsewhere on this site, with targeted web research to fill the gaps the papers don't cover.

## What this blog is

This blog complements the per-paper summaries and conference overviews that make up the rest of the site. Where those aim for faithful, structured paraphrases of a single paper, a blog post takes a **perspective** — a topic the user cares about (say, "the quiet return of log-structured storage," or "why every LLM serving paper is really a cache paper") — and weaves together evidence from multiple papers plus targeted web research into a single argument.

## How posts are produced

Each post is commissioned by the user with a perspective prompt. A coding agent then:

1. Reads the relevant indexed paper summaries and the source PDFs when needed.
2. Does targeted web search to pick up context that isn't in the papers — industry benchmarks, blog-post counter-arguments, follow-up work.
3. Drafts the essay in both English and Simplified Chinese, with inline links to the paper pages on this site and to external sources.

See [`prompts/blog-writing.md`](https://github.com/) for the full methodology.

## What to expect

Posts will land here as topics come up. Everything is dated and tagged; nothing here is finalized until the `draft` flag is cleared.
