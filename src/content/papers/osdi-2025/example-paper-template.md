## TL;DR

One- to three-sentence punchline. What does this paper do, and why should the reader care?
Skip adjectives; name the mechanism and the win.

## Problem

What is broken or missing in the prior art? Why does this problem matter — who has it, at
what scale, and what goes wrong if nothing changes? Name the concrete failure modes of the
most obvious existing approaches.

## Key Insight

The one idea a reader should remember in six months. State it as a claim, not a description
of the paper. Explain *why* the insight works before *how* it is implemented.

## Design

Mechanism, architecture, algorithms. Identify any load-bearing invariants. Include pseudocode
only when it is genuinely the clearest expression of the idea. If there is a data-path /
control-path split, describe each.

## Evaluation

Setup (testbed, workloads), baselines, and the two or three numbers that matter. Comment on
whether the evaluation actually supports the central claim — e.g. do the workloads exercise
the stated bottleneck? Are baselines configured fairly?

## Novelty & Impact

How does this differ from the closest prior work (by a sentence each)? Who will cite this
and why? Is it a new mechanism, a new problem framing, or a strong measurement study?

## Limitations

Author-stated limits plus reviewer-style concerns. Is there a workload regime where the
design would lose? A deployment constraint the authors hand-wave?
