OSDI '25 brought **53 papers** in a single-track program whose center of gravity has shifted decisively toward ML systems — 16 of the 53 papers are directly about large-model serving, training, or ML-kernel tooling — yet the classical OSDI portfolio remains intact, with strong showings in memory/storage, distributed transactions, verification, and kernels. The year's defining tension is between *fine-grained decomposition* (disaggregated memory, CXL pods, tiered memory, erasure-coded placement, role-decomposed NIC rings) and *speculative recomposition* (speculate-first protocols and searches that reconcile only if the guess was wrong). Two unusual additions to the program — a pair of quantum-OS papers and a methodology paper distilling serial performance optimization — round out a program that is unusually self-conscious about how systems work is done, not just what is built.

## Themes

- **LLM serving and training are first-class systems workloads, not niches.** Serving-side work covers O(1) autoscaling with weight multicast in [BlitzScale](../papers/osdi-2025/blitzscale-fast-and-live-large-model-autoscaling-with-o-1-host-caching.md), auto-searched nano-batch pipelining in [NanoFlow](../papers/osdi-2025/nanoflow-towards-optimal-large-language-model-serving-throughput.md), wafer-scale mesh mapping in [WaferLLM](../papers/osdi-2025/waferllm-large-language-model-inference-at-wafer-scale.md), and near-hidden low-bit recovery in [DecDEC](../papers/osdi-2025/decdec-a-systems-approach-to-advancing-low-bit-llm-quantization.md). On the training side, [WLB-LLM](../papers/osdi-2025/wlb-llm-workload-balanced-4d-parallelism-for-large-language-model-training.md) rebalances 4D parallelism around variable-length workloads, [ZEN](../papers/osdi-2025/zen-empowering-distributed-training-with-sparsity-driven-data-synchronization.md) finds a near-optimal sparse-gradient sync plan, [Understanding Stragglers](../papers/osdi-2025/understanding-stragglers-in-large-model-training-using-what-if-analysis.md) introduces what-if analysis over training traces, and [TrainCheck](../papers/osdi-2025/training-with-confidence-catching-silent-errors-in-deep-learning-training-with-automated.md) catches silent errors before metrics drift.

- **Accelerator kernels become compiler IR rather than opaque blobs.** [KPerfIR](../papers/osdi-2025/kperfir-towards-a-open-and-compiler-centric-ecosystem-for-gpu-kernel-performance-tooling.md) expresses GPU profiling as Triton IR passes, [NEUTRINO](../papers/osdi-2025/neutrino-fine-grained-gpu-kernel-profiling-via-programmable-probing.md) injects programmable probes at the assembly level, [Mirage](../papers/osdi-2025/mirage-a-multi-level-superoptimizer-for-tensor-programs.md) searches across kernel/block/thread levels with probabilistic verification, [QiMeng-Xpiler](../papers/osdi-2025/qimeng-xpiler-transcompiling-tensor-programs-for-deep-learning-systems-with-a-neural.md) combines LLM-generated sketches with SMT repair to transpile between CUDA, HIP, BANG, and VNNI, and [PipeThreader](../papers/osdi-2025/pipethreader-software-defined-pipelining-for-efficient-dnn-execution.md) software-schedules TMA/TensorCore/CUDA-core pipelines from explicit sTask graphs.

- **Memory and storage shift from "give me more" to "give me finer control".** [FineMem](../papers/osdi-2025/finemem-breaking-the-allocation-overhead-vs-memory-waste-dilemma-in-fine-grained.md) decouples RDMA registration from protection and allocation, [Tigon](../papers/osdi-2025/tigon-a-distributed-database-for-a-cxl-pod.md) keeps only cross-host active tuples in shared CXL memory, [AOL](../papers/osdi-2025/tiered-memory-management-beyond-hotness.md) replaces hotness with latency/MLP-aware impact, [EMT](../papers/osdi-2025/emt-an-os-framework-for-new-memory-translation-architectures.md) refactors Linux translation around pluggable MMU drivers, [Okapi](../papers/osdi-2025/okapi-decoupling-data-striping-and-redundancy-grouping-in-cluster-file-systems.md) decouples stripe width from erasure-code group width, and [Nos](../papers/osdi-2025/stripeless-data-placement-for-erasure-coded-in-memory-storage.md) eliminates stripes entirely via combinatorial backup selection.

- **Verification leaves the lab: runtime checks, provenance proofs, and determinism in untrusted code.** [Basilisk](../papers/osdi-2025/basilisk-using-provenance-invariants-to-automate-proofs-of-undecidable-protocols.md) automates proofs of undecidable distributed protocols via atomic sharding, [PoWER](../papers/osdi-2025/power-never-corrupts-tool-agnostic-verification-of-crash-consistency-and-corruption.md) verifies PM crash consistency and corruption handling in both Verus and Dafny, [T2C](../papers/osdi-2025/deriving-semantic-checkers-from-tests-to-detect-silent-failures-in-production-distributed.md) turns existing system tests into runtime semantic checkers, [DeCl](../papers/osdi-2025/deterministic-client-enforcing-determinism-on-untrusted-machine-code.md) statically verifies x86-64/Arm64 machine code for deterministic execution without a trusted JIT, and [Omniglot](../papers/osdi-2025/building-bridges-safe-interactions-with-foreign-languages-through-omniglot.md) makes Rust's FFI sound without giving up zero-copy sharing.

- **Heterogeneous scheduling and profiling catch up with hardware diversity.** [XSched](../papers/osdi-2025/xsched-preemptive-scheduling-for-diverse-xpus.md) introduces one preemptible scheduling abstraction across GPUs, NPUs, ASICs, and FPGAs, [Tintin](../papers/osdi-2025/tintin-a-unified-hardware-performance-profiling-infrastructure-to-uncover-and-manage.md) treats HW-counter multiplexing error as first-class uncertainty, and two papers — [QOS](../papers/osdi-2025/qos-quantum-operating-system.md) and [HyperQ](../papers/osdi-2025/quantum-virtual-machines.md) — apply the same "schedulable cloud resource" framing to quantum processors, pointing at an emerging systems-for-quantum subfield.

## Notable trends

- **Speculate early, reconcile later.** A consistent technique across layers: [Mako](../papers/osdi-2025/mako-speculative-distributed-transactions-with-geo-replication.md) certifies cross-shard transactions before WAN replication finishes, [SpecLog](../papers/osdi-2025/low-end-to-end-latency-atop-a-speculative-shared-log-with-fix-ante-ordering.md) delivers shared-log records before global ordering completes, [PipeANN](../papers/osdi-2025/achieving-low-latency-graph-based-vector-search-via-aligning-best-first-search-algorithm.md) pipelines speculative SSD reads during graph traversal, and [Tigon](../papers/osdi-2025/tigon-a-distributed-database-for-a-cxl-pod.md) uses DB-aware software coherence to commit multi-partition transactions without 2PC. Each trades rare rollback cost for common-case latency.

- **Role-decomposition rather than new mechanisms.** Several papers take an interface that historically bundled two roles and split it: [FineMem](../papers/osdi-2025/finemem-breaking-the-allocation-overhead-vs-memory-waste-dilemma-in-fine-grained.md) decouples RDMA registration / protection / allocation, [rxBisect](../papers/osdi-2025/disentangling-the-dual-role-of-nic-receive-rings.md) splits the NIC Rx ring into allocation and reception rings, [Okapi](../papers/osdi-2025/okapi-decoupling-data-striping-and-redundancy-grouping-in-cluster-file-systems.md) decouples striping width from redundancy grouping, and [Omniglot](../papers/osdi-2025/building-bridges-safe-interactions-with-foreign-languages-through-omniglot.md) factors FFI safety into sandbox + typestate + scope.

- **Derive checks from existing artifacts.** Instead of hand-writing invariants, several papers harvest them: [T2C](../papers/osdi-2025/deriving-semantic-checkers-from-tests-to-detect-silent-failures-in-production-distributed.md) slices assertions out of tests, [TrainCheck](../papers/osdi-2025/training-with-confidence-catching-silent-errors-in-deep-learning-training-with-automated.md) infers training-pipeline invariants from examples, [Basilisk](../papers/osdi-2025/basilisk-using-provenance-invariants-to-automate-proofs-of-undecidable-protocols.md) synthesizes inductive invariants from provenance, and [Paralegal](../papers/osdi-2025/paralegal-practical-static-analysis-for-privacy-bugs.md) checks privacy policies against program-dependence graphs derived via ownership-aware types.

- **Network and DMA paths get scoped down, not scaled up.** [Söze](../papers/osdi-2025/s-ze-one-network-telemetry-is-all-you-need-for-per-flow-weighted-bandwidth-allocation-at.md) gets per-flow weighted max-min allocation from one per-packet delay signal, [rxBisect](../papers/osdi-2025/disentangling-the-dual-role-of-nic-receive-rings.md) shrinks the DDIO working set by isolating the burst-absorption ring, and [VIO](../papers/osdi-2025/to-pri-or-not-to-pri-that-s-the-question.md) removes I/O page faults from the DMA critical path by snooping VirtIO requests. The common move is dropping mechanism, not adding it.

## Must-read picks

- **[Basilisk](../papers/osdi-2025/basilisk-using-provenance-invariants-to-automate-proofs-of-undecidable-protocols.md)** — Best Paper; atomic sharding turns global inductive-invariant synthesis for undecidable distributed protocols into a practical Dafny workflow.
- **[FineMem](../papers/osdi-2025/finemem-breaking-the-allocation-overhead-vs-memory-waste-dilemma-in-fine-grained.md)** — an allocator substrate for disaggregated memory: ~1.3 CAS/alloc vs. 45 for prior one-sided designs, 2.25–2.8× utilization at 2.5–4.1% overhead — useful as a building block under malloc, KV, and swap systems alike.
- **[NanoFlow](../papers/osdi-2025/nanoflow-towards-optimal-large-language-model-serving-throughput.md)** — automates the nano-batch search that overlaps compute / memory / network kernels inside a single GPU instance, a directly adoptable throughput win for LLM serving stacks.
- **[Mako](../papers/osdi-2025/mako-speculative-distributed-transactions-with-geo-replication.md)** — a clean generalization of speculate-and-reconcile: cross-shard transactions commit before WAN replication, with vector watermarks bounding rollback after shard failures.
- **[KPerfIR](../papers/osdi-2025/kperfir-towards-a-open-and-compiler-centric-ecosystem-for-gpu-kernel-performance-tooling.md)** — community infrastructure: GPU kernel profiling reframed as Triton IR passes, enabling FA3-level optimizations that vendor-blackbox tools cannot explain.
