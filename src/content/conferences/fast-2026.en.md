FAST '26 brought **44 papers** to Santa Clara — a storage-dominated program,
but one refracted through the two pressures that define the current moment:
AI workloads reshaping the storage path, and new device classes (CXL-SSD,
FDP, zoned UFS, high-bandwidth all-flash arrays) running ahead of the
Linux-era I/O stack that was meant to serve them. Industrial systems papers
from Apple, Alibaba, ByteDance, Samsung, Huawei, and SK hynix make up an
unusually large share this year — FAST has always been industry-friendly,
but this edition is especially heavy on production-scale cost, reliability,
and deployment engineering.

## Themes

**AI is redrawing the storage stack.** Eight of the 44 papers sit explicitly in an AI-era-storage track, and several more touch it sideways. The pattern is that LLM and ML workloads aren't just new tenants: they force redesigns of the storage path. [SolidAttention](../papers/fast-2026/solidattention-low-latency-ssd-based-serving-on-memory-constrained-pcs.md) co-designs sparse attention with SSD layout and DAG scheduling for local 128k serving; [CacheSlide](../papers/fast-2026/cacheslide-unlocking-cross-position-aware-kv-cache-reuse-for-accelerating-llm-serving.md) and [Bidaw](../papers/fast-2026/bidaw-enhancing-key-value-caching-for-interactive-llm-serving-via-bidirectional-computatio.md) chase cross-position KV reuse and bidirectional compute-storage KV placement for cloud serving; [a programmable page cache for LLM model loading](../papers/fast-2026/accelerating-model-loading-in-llm-inference-by-programmable-page-cache.md) and [AITurbo's grouped I/O API](../papers/fast-2026/fast-cloud-storage-for-ai-jobs-via-grouped-i-o-api-with-transparent-read-write-optimizatio.md) push AI-shaped access patterns back into the storage stack; [AdaCheck](../papers/fast-2026/adacheck-an-adaptive-checkpointing-system-for-efficient-llm-training-with-redundancy-utili.md) and [GCR](../papers/fast-2026/gpu-checkpoint-restore-made-fast-and-lightweight.md) make per-step LLM training and GPU checkpoints affordable; [Seneca](../papers/fast-2026/preparation-meets-opportunity-enhancing-data-preprocessing-for-ml-training-with-seneca.md) rewrites the ML preprocessing cache itself.

**Specializing the I/O path for faster, denser devices.** A second cluster rebuilds Linux-era primitives so they keep up with the devices underneath them. [Rearchitecting buffered I/O](../papers/fast-2026/rearchitecting-buffered-i-o-in-the-era-of-high-bandwidth-ssds.md) replaces the all-the-way page cache with a scrap buffer for high-bandwidth SSD arrays; [UnICom](../papers/fast-2026/unicom-a-universally-high-performant-i-o-completion-mechanism-for-modern-computer-systems.md) moves direct-I/O completion into the kernel; [DPAS](../papers/fast-2026/dpas-a-prompt-accurate-and-safe-i-o-completion-method-for-ssds.md) learns per-I/O sleep timing for SSDs; [ScaleSwap](../papers/fast-2026/scaleswap-a-scalable-os-swap-system-for-all-flash-swap-arrays.md) redesigns swap around per-core files and delegated metadata; [uCache](../papers/fast-2026/ucache-a-customizable-unikernel-based-io-cache.md) makes an `mmap`-style IO cache customizable in a unikernel; [ZUFS](../papers/fast-2026/unleashing-zoned-ufs-cross-layer-optimizations-for-next-generation-mobile-storage.md) finally makes zoned UFS shippable on phones; [Advancing Data Integrity in Linux](../papers/fast-2026/advancing-data-integrity-in-linux.md) pushes PI through io_uring and file systems.

**Simulating hardware that hasn't shipped.** CXL and next-gen SSD standards are still mostly on paper, and this FAST is unusual in that a whole sub-theme is about letting researchers study those devices before silicon arrives. [Xerxes](../papers/fast-2026/xerxes-extensive-exploration-of-scalable-hardware-systems-with-cxl-based-simulation-framew.md) simulates CXL 3.1 fabrics with device-managed coherence; [Cylon](../papers/fast-2026/cylon-fast-and-accurate-full-system-emulation-of-cxl-ssds.md) remaps EPT entries so CXL-SSD hits execute as direct loads; [WARP](../papers/fast-2026/characterizing-and-emulating-fdp-ssds-with-warp.md) characterizes FDP SSDs and turns hidden controller choices into explicit knobs.

**Industrial cost engineering at scale.** FAST has always had an industry streak, and this year it's unusually concentrated. [McQueen](../papers/fast-2026/mcqueen-apple-s-geo-distributed-object-store-at-exabyte-scale.md) drops Apple's object-store replication factor from 2.40 to 1.50 via five-region XOR segmentation; [PolarStore](../papers/fast-2026/polarstore-high-performance-data-compression-for-large-scale-cloud-native-databases.md) achieves 60% lower storage cost on PolarDB by pairing 16 KB software compression with byte-granular CSD compression; [DisCoGC](../papers/fast-2026/discard-based-garbage-collection-for-distributed-log-structured-storage-systems-in-bytedan.md) cuts ByteDance's production TCO by ~20% with lightweight discards over log-structured ByteStore; [TapeOBS](../papers/fast-2026/cost-efficient-archive-cloud-storage-with-tape-design-and-deployment.md) models a 4.95x 10-year TCO cut by staging through HDDs before tape; [RASK](../papers/fast-2026/range-as-a-key-is-the-key-fast-and-compact-cloud-block-store-index-with-rask.md) shaves 98.9% of cloud block-store index memory; an invited-style [retrospective on local storage in cloud](../papers/fast-2026/here-there-and-everywhere-the-past-the-present-and-the-future-of-local-storage-in-cloud.md) proposes a local-plus-EBS hybrid for the next generation.

**Smarter placement and repair in log-structured and erasure-coded systems.** A cluster of papers applies better policy to already-shipped storage shapes. [DOGI](../papers/fast-2026/dogi-data-placement-with-oracle-guided-insights-for-log-structured-systems.md) uses an oracle-inspired hot filter for log-structured placement; [HATS](../papers/fast-2026/holistic-and-automated-task-scheduling-for-distributed-lsm-tree-based-storage.md) combines replica assignment, request rerouting, and read-driven compaction for distributed LSM; [DRBoost](../papers/fast-2026/drboost-boosting-degraded-read-performance-in-msr-coded-storage-clusters.md) reconstructs only needed sub-chunks in MSR-coded stores; [LESS](../papers/fast-2026/less-is-more-for-i-o-efficient-repairs-in-erasure-coded-storage.md) layers a few RS-coded extended sub-stripes to bring EC-repair I/O down without Clay-style seek blow-up; [ParaSync](../papers/fast-2026/parasync-exploiting-fine-grained-parallelism-for-efficient-file-synchronization.md) and [SkySync](../papers/fast-2026/skysync-accelerating-file-synchronization-with-collaborative-delta-generation.md) parallelize and reuse checksums for CDC-style file sync.

## Notable trends

- **Block-wise sparse attention is being re-hosted on SSDs.** At least three papers — [SolidAttention](../papers/fast-2026/solidattention-low-latency-ssd-based-serving-on-memory-constrained-pcs.md), [CacheSlide](../papers/fast-2026/cacheslide-unlocking-cross-position-aware-kv-cache-reuse-for-accelerating-llm-serving.md), and [Bidaw](../papers/fast-2026/bidaw-enhancing-key-value-caching-for-interactive-llm-serving-via-bidirectional-computatio.md) — argue that attention's selection unit and storage's transfer unit should not be the same thing, and co-design the two so 128k-context decode works without keeping the KV cache in memory.
- **Production deployments are headlining.** Apple, Alibaba, ByteDance, and Samsung each contribute at-scale systems papers — [McQueen](../papers/fast-2026/mcqueen-apple-s-geo-distributed-object-store-at-exabyte-scale.md), [PolarStore](../papers/fast-2026/polarstore-high-performance-data-compression-for-large-scale-cloud-native-databases.md), [DisCoGC](../papers/fast-2026/discard-based-garbage-collection-for-distributed-log-structured-storage-systems-in-bytedan.md), and the [FailureMiner](../papers/fast-2026/failureminer-a-joint-key-decision-mining-scheme-for-practical-ssd-failure-prediction-and-a.md) threshold-mining work. The ratio of industry-led papers is higher than it tends to be in pure academic systems venues this year.
- **CXL-class and FDP devices are simulated first, evaluated second.** [Xerxes](../papers/fast-2026/xerxes-extensive-exploration-of-scalable-hardware-systems-with-cxl-based-simulation-framew.md), [Cylon](../papers/fast-2026/cylon-fast-and-accurate-full-system-emulation-of-cxl-ssds.md), and [WARP](../papers/fast-2026/characterizing-and-emulating-fdp-ssds-with-warp.md) all make emulation a first-class research output, reflecting how far ahead these standards are running of shipping silicon.
- **Per-core and per-request primitives replace global schedulers.** [ScaleSwap](../papers/fast-2026/scaleswap-a-scalable-os-swap-system-for-all-flash-swap-arrays.md) shards swap per-core, [UnICom](../papers/fast-2026/unicom-a-universally-high-performant-i-o-completion-mechanism-for-modern-computer-systems.md) uses scheduler-tagged completion paths, and [Lockify](../papers/fast-2026/lockify-understanding-linux-distributed-lock-management-overheads-in-shared-storage.md) lets file creators self-declare initial lock ownership instead of hitting the DLM. When the device is fast, the bottleneck is always a shared path in the OS.

## Must-read picks

- **[SolidAttention: Low-Latency SSD-based Serving on Memory-Constrained PCs](../papers/fast-2026/solidattention-low-latency-ssd-based-serving-on-memory-constrained-pcs.md)** — Cleanest AI × storage co-design in the program: interleaved KV layout, speculative prefetch with overwrite correction, and DAG scheduling, quantified at 3.1x throughput and ~98% KV-memory reduction for local 128k inference.
- **[McQueen: Apple's Geo-Distributed Object Store at Exabyte Scale](../papers/fast-2026/mcqueen-apple-s-geo-distributed-object-store-at-exabyte-scale.md)** — Real exabyte-scale deployment that moves from dual-region replication to five-region XOR segmentation, replication factor 2.40 → 1.50. Rare end-to-end industrial design paper.
- **[PolarStore: High-Performance Data Compression for Large-Scale Cloud-Native Databases](../papers/fast-2026/polarstore-high-performance-data-compression-for-large-scale-cloud-native-databases.md)** — Alibaba Cloud's answer to "where does the storage cost go" for PolarDB, combining 16 KB software compression with byte-granular CSD compression and DB-specific fast paths for a 3.55x ratio and ~60% storage-cost reduction.
- **[Advancing Data Integrity in Linux](../papers/fast-2026/advancing-data-integrity-in-linux.md)** — Samsung/EPFL upstream work extending Linux PI with io_uring metadata paths and filesystem-managed PI. Likely to affect storage stacks well beyond this paper's own benchmarks.
- **[Xerxes: Extensive Exploration of Scalable Hardware Systems with CXL-Based Simulation Framework](../papers/fast-2026/xerxes-extensive-exploration-of-scalable-hardware-systems-with-cxl-based-simulation-framew.md)** — Community-infrastructure paper for CXL 3.1 fabrics with device-managed coherence: the piece of tooling many other storage/memory papers will build on over the next couple of years.
