SOSP '25 brought **66 papers** to Seoul, with ML systems now a plurality rather than a minority: 17 of the 66 papers target LLM serving or LLM training infrastructure directly, and another 6 sit in GPU / accelerator plumbing that exists mostly because of ML. The classical SOSP backbone is still present — kernels, verified systems, memory and storage, distributed databases, scheduling — but the program's center of gravity has visibly moved toward *substrates that virtualize GPUs and remote memory for ML workloads*. A second, quieter thread runs through the rest of the program: verification is becoming a routine tool rather than a heroic effort, showing up in kernels, eBPF extensions, hypervisors, training plans, filesystems, and WASI runtimes. The year's defining pair of tensions is therefore **substrate vs. serving trick** on the ML side, and **verify-once vs. check-at-runtime** on the reliability side.

## Themes

- **LLM serving and GPU substrates are converging on a shared set of primitives.** [Aegaeon](../papers/sosp-2025/aegaeon-effective-gpu-pooling-for-concurrent-llm-serving-on-the-market.md) autoscales at token boundaries to pool marketplace models on shared GPUs; [Jenga](../papers/sosp-2025/jenga-effective-memory-management-for-serving-llm-with-heterogeneity.md) and [DiffKV](../papers/sosp-2025/diffkv-differentiated-memory-management-for-large-language-models-with-parallel-kv.md) redesign KV memory for heterogeneous models and asymmetric K/V sensitivity; [Pie](../papers/sosp-2025/pie-a-programmable-serving-system-for-emerging-llm-applications.md) exposes KV pages and generation steps as an inferlet programming model; [PrefillOnly](../papers/sosp-2025/prefillonly-an-inference-engine-for-prefill-only-workloads-in-large-language-model.md) specializes for single-token prefill requests; and [LithOS](../papers/sosp-2025/lithos-an-operating-system-for-efficient-machine-learning-on-gpus.md) places the whole control plane under the CUDA driver boundary with TPC-granular scheduling and kernel atomization. Together they argue that serving systems now need an OS-style substrate rather than ad-hoc schedulers.

- **Retrieval, dynamics, and hybrid hardware push LLMs beyond the homogeneous-GPU model.** [HedraRAG](../papers/sosp-2025/hedrarag-co-optimizing-generation-and-retrieval-for-heterogeneous-rag-workflows.md) and [METIS](../papers/sosp-2025/metis-fast-quality-aware-rag-systems-with-configuration-adaptation.md) co-optimize retrieval and generation; [IC-Cache](../papers/sosp-2025/ic-cache-efficient-large-language-model-serving-via-in-context-caching.md) reuses past request-response pairs as in-context demonstrations to route across small and large models; [KTransformers](../papers/sosp-2025/ktransformers-unleashing-the-full-potential-of-cpu-gpu-hybrid-inference-for-moe-models.md) runs large MoE inference across CPU AMX and GPU; [HeteroInfer](../papers/sosp-2025/characterizing-mobile-soc-for-accelerating-heterogeneous-llm-inference.md) partitions LLM work across mobile GPU and NPU; [Tempo](../papers/sosp-2025/tempo-compiled-dynamic-deep-learning-with-symbolic-dependence-graphs.md) makes time an explicit tensor dimension for dynamic LLM/RL workloads.

- **Training infrastructure has hardened into systems work in its own right.** [ByteRobust](../papers/sosp-2025/robust-llm-training-infrastructure-at-bytedance.md) chronicles a 10k-GPU production stack with isolation-first fault handling; [Mycroft](../papers/sosp-2025/mycroft-tracing-dependencies-in-collective-communication-towards-reliable-llm-training.md) traces collective dependencies to localize hangs in seconds; [Sailor](../papers/sosp-2025/sailor-automating-distributed-training-over-dynamic-heterogeneous-and-geo-distributed.md) jointly picks allocation, 3D parallelism, and zone placement under churn; [DCP](../papers/sosp-2025/dcp-addressing-input-dynamism-in-long-context-training-via-dynamic-context-parallelism.md) replaces fixed context-parallel layouts with per-batch block placement; and [TrainVerify](../papers/sosp-2025/trainverify-equivalence-based-verification-for-distributed-llm-training.md) proves a distributed training plan matches the logical model. Reliability, verification, and scheduling all meet here.

- **Verification leaves the toolbox and enters the kernel, verifier, and hypervisor.** [Atmosphere](../papers/sosp-2025/atmosphere-practical-verified-kernels-with-rust-and-verus.md) proves a full-featured microkernel in Rust+Verus with raw-pointer code and flat permission maps; [BCF](../papers/sosp-2025/prove-it-to-the-kernel-precise-extension-analysis-via-proof-guided-abstraction-refinement.md) offloads hard eBPF refinement proofs to user space and checks them in linear time, recovering 403 wrongly rejected programs; [TickTock](../papers/sosp-2025/ticktock-verified-isolation-in-a-production-embedded-os.md) replaces Tock's monolithic MPU abstraction with a Flux-verified granular design; [CortenMM](../papers/sosp-2025/cortenmm-efficient-memory-management-with-strong-correctness-guarantees.md) verifies the locking core of a range-transactional memory manager; [Ghost in the Android Shell](../papers/sosp-2025/ghost-in-the-android-shell-pragmatic-test-oracle-specification-of-a-production-hypervisor.md) embeds executable ghost-state specs inside pKVM; and [AutoMan](../papers/sosp-2025/automan-facilitating-verified-distributed-systems-development-through-automatic-code.md) turns Dafny actions into verified code with developer-owned hot paths.

- **Memory keeps decomposing into tiers, remote pools, and interdependent resources.** [Scalable Far Memory](../papers/sosp-2025/scalable-far-memory-balancing-faults-and-evictions.md) fully decouples eviction from faulting; [Spirit](../papers/sosp-2025/spirit-fair-allocation-of-interdependent-resources-in-remote-memory-systems.md) jointly prices local cache and remote bandwidth; [Demeter](../papers/sosp-2025/demeter-a-scalable-and-elastic-tiered-memory-solution-for-virtualized-cloud-via-guest.md) moves VM tiering into the guest with guest-driven PEBS; [cache_ext](../papers/sosp-2025/cache-ext-customizing-the-page-cache-with-ebpf.md) puts per-cgroup eviction policies behind an eBPF interface; [How to Copy Memory](../papers/sosp-2025/how-to-copy-memory-coordinated-asynchronous-copy-as-a-first-class-os-service.md) makes memory copy an OS-managed asynchronous service; and [Scalable Address Spaces](../papers/sosp-2025/scalable-address-spaces-using-concurrent-interval-skiplist.md) replaces coarse mmap locks with a concurrent interval skiplist.

## Notable trends

- **Push the policy point below the vendor driver or framework.** LLM and ML systems repeatedly place their control plane just underneath an ossified API: [LithOS](../papers/sosp-2025/lithos-an-operating-system-for-efficient-machine-learning-on-gpus.md) interposes on CUDA's driver API; [PhoenixOS](../papers/sosp-2025/phoenixos-concurrent-os-level-gpu-checkpoint-and-restore-with-validated-speculation.md) does concurrent OS-level GPU C/R with speculated-then-validated access sets; [Mercury](../papers/sosp-2025/mercury-unlocking-multi-gpu-operator-optimization-for-llms-via-remote-memory-scheduling.md) schedules across GPU memory as remote storage; [GoFS](../papers/sosp-2025/managing-scalable-direct-storage-accesses-for-gpus-with-gofs.md) moves F2FS metadata and NVMe queue control onto the GPU; [cache_ext](../papers/sosp-2025/cache-ext-customizing-the-page-cache-with-ebpf.md) and [FlexGuard](../papers/sosp-2025/flexguard-fast-mutual-exclusion-independent-of-subscription.md) turn eBPF hooks into customizable kernel policy points. The common move is to avoid forking the framework or the vendor stack while still taking control.

- **Predict the common case, validate and recover on the rare case.** [Tiga](../papers/sosp-2025/tiga-accelerating-geo-distributed-transactions-with-synchronized-clocks.md) predicts a future timestamp for geo-tx ordering and validates with super-quorum log checks; [Pesto](../papers/sosp-2025/pesto-cooking-up-high-performance-bft-queries.md) skips global ordering for non-conflicting BFT SQL; [PhoenixOS](../papers/sosp-2025/phoenixos-concurrent-os-level-gpu-checkpoint-and-restore-with-validated-speculation.md) speculates GPU kernel access sets from launch arguments; [Optimistic Recovery (Phoenix)](../papers/sosp-2025/optimistic-recovery-for-high-availability-software-via-partial-process-state-preservation.md) restarts from main while reusing selected long-lived state; [Orthrus](../papers/sosp-2025/orthrus-efficient-and-timely-detection-of-silent-user-data-corruption-in-the-cloud-with.md) validates data-path closures on other cores. Each paper trades a small rollback/cross-check cost for big common-case wins.

- **Shrink the programming model rather than broaden it.** [Dandelion](../papers/sosp-2025/unlocking-true-elasticity-for-the-cloud-native-era-with-dandelion.md) replaces POSIX serverless sandboxes with DAGs of pure compute functions; [Quilt](../papers/sosp-2025/quilt-resource-aware-merging-of-serverless-workflows.md) merges profitable serverless subgraphs into one LLVM-level process; [µFork](../papers/sosp-2025/fork-supporting-posix-fork-within-a-single-address-space-os.md) emulates POSIX fork inside a single CHERI address space; [Pie](../papers/sosp-2025/pie-a-programmable-serving-system-for-emerging-llm-applications.md) replaces the fixed LLM serving loop with inferlets. Each restricts the API so the runtime can specialize aggressively.

- **Derive hard things from existing artifacts.** [KNighter](../papers/sosp-2025/knighter-transforming-static-analysis-with-llm-synthesized-checkers.md) synthesizes Clang Static Analyzer checkers from bug-fix patches with LLM triage; [Fawkes](../papers/sosp-2025/fawkes-finding-data-durability-bugs-in-dbmss-via-recovered-data-state-verification.md) builds a checkpoint-rectified state graph from DBMS traces; [WASIT](../papers/sosp-2025/wasit-deep-and-continuous-differential-testing-of-webassembly-system-interface.md) turns the WASI spec into executable constraints and abstract resource states; [Veritas](../papers/sosp-2025/ebpf-misbehavior-detection-fuzzing-with-a-specification-based-oracle.md) pairs eBPF fuzzing with a Dafny specification oracle. Invariants and oracles come from code and specs that already exist, not from new hand-written ones.

## Must-read picks

- **[LithOS](../papers/sosp-2025/lithos-an-operating-system-for-efficient-machine-learning-on-gpus.md)** — makes the case that GPU OS work needs a substrate, not more point schedulers: TPC-granular scheduling, transparent kernel atomization, right-sizing, and DVFS all unified under the CUDA driver boundary.
- **[Aegaeon](../papers/sosp-2025/aegaeon-effective-gpu-pooling-for-concurrent-llm-serving-on-the-market.md)** — the clearest concrete datapoint for token-boundary autoscaling on a model marketplace: 2-2.5× higher arrival rates, 82% GPU reduction in beta at Alibaba.
- **[Atmosphere](../papers/sosp-2025/atmosphere-practical-verified-kernels-with-rust-and-verus.md)** — verified kernels made practical: raw-pointer Rust with Verus, flat permission maps, and explicit allocation prove refinement, safety, and noninterference for a real separation kernel.
- **[Tiga](../papers/sosp-2025/tiga-accelerating-geo-distributed-transactions-with-synchronized-clocks.md)** — a clean primitive for geo-distributed OLTP: synchronized clocks and measured one-way delays let most conflicting transactions commit in 1 WRTT, with a well-defined validation fallback.
- **[Dandelion](../papers/sosp-2025/unlocking-true-elasticity-for-the-cloud-native-era-with-dandelion.md)** — rethinks serverless elasticity by shrinking the programming model to DAGs of pure compute + trusted communication, enabling per-request cold starts in hundreds of microseconds.
